Type of cyberattacks

Cyberattacks are malicious attempts to exploit vulnerabilities in systems, networks, or devices. These attacks aim to disrupt, damage, steal, or gain unauthorized access to data and systems. Below are some common types of cyberattacks:

1. Malware Attacks

Malware (malicious software) is designed to harm or exploit devices, networks, or systems.

  • Viruses: Attach to files and spread to other systems.
  • Worms: Self-replicating programs that spread across networks.
  • Ransomware: Locks or encrypts data, demanding payment to restore access.
  • Spyware: Collects user information without consent.
  • Trojans: Masquerade as legitimate software but perform malicious actions.

2. Phishing

Phishing involves tricking individuals into revealing sensitive information by posing as a trustworthy entity.

  • Email Phishing: Fraudulent emails containing malicious links or attachments.
  • Spear Phishing: Targeted phishing tailored to a specific individual or organization.
  • Smishing and Vishing: Phishing via SMS (smishing) or voice calls (vishing).

3. Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) Attacks

An attacker intercepts communication between two parties to steal or manipulate data.

  • Eavesdropping on encrypted connections.
  • Spoofing Wi-Fi networks to steal user credentials.
