DARKWEB

The Dark Web is a part of the internet that is not indexed by standard search engines like Google, Bing, or Yahoo. It exists on darknets, which are overlay networks requiring specific software, configurations, or authorizations to access. The dark web is often associated with anonymity and privacy, as it uses encryption tools and anonymizing networks to conceal users' identities and activities.

Structure of the Web

  1. Surface Web: The visible portion of the internet accessible through standard browsers and search engines.
    • Examples: Websites like Wikipedia, news portals, and social media.
  2. Deep Web: Content not indexed by search engines, including private databases, academic resources, and intranets.
    • Examples: Online banking portals, subscription services, medical records.
  3. Dark Web: A subset of the deep web that is deliberately hidden and accessible only with special software like Tor or I2P.
